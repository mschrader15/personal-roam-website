Monte-Carlo Simulation and SUMO

  • After reading A two-level probabilistic approach for validation of stochastic traffic simulations..., I decided that my next paper needed to take a fundamentally more probabilistic approach. Before we get into The How, we need to dive into the Why

  • Why Monte-Carlo

    • [[Traffic]] Simulation has a lot of uncertainty. The goal is to model the collective behavior of thousands of vehicles. Each vehicle has its own car-following model and lane-changing model (each of which is inherently under-constrained).

      • The difference between each actual driver and the simulated driver is error, but to know each individuals' driving characteristics in the population would be impossible.

    • In fact, there is both the aleatory uncertainty and epistemic uncertainty components

      • This figure from 1 is a great visual representation of the sources of uncertainty
